Discover Your Community: An Overview of Activities at the Newcomers Club of Greater Park City

1. Welcome to the Club!

On behalf of the Welcoming Committee, we are thrilled to have you here! The Newcomers Club of Greater Park City is built on a simple and powerful motto: Making friends, having fun, creating memories. Whether you've just moved to the area or are a long-time resident looking to expand your social circle, you've come to the right place. This document is your guide to the wide variety of activity groups we offer. Our goal is to provide a snapshot of each group so you can easily find your fit, connect with others who share your interests, and start creating those memories.

2. Get Your Game On: Groups for Cards, Dice & Strategy

These groups are perfect for members who enjoy friendly competition, strategic thinking, and socializing over a great game.

2.1. American Mah Jongg

This is a new and exciting activity for the club. We welcome players of all backgrounds to join us for this classic game of skill and luck.

* Meeting Time: Tuesdays, 9:30 am to 11:30 am
* Location: Park City Library
* Key Benefit: To ensure everyone has a great time, tables are organized by experience level. For those new to the game, we make an effort to include instruction and mentoring for beginners.

2.2. Bridge Clubs

We offer two distinct bridge groups to suit different play styles and skill levels.

Duplicate Bridge	Fun Bridge
Best For:	Intermediate and advanced players.
Vibe:	A fun group that uses a traditional duplicate format.
Details:	Uses bidding boxes, boards, and duplicate scoring sheets.
Meeting Time:	A weekly email is sent out for players to sign up.
Location:	Announced with weekly signup.

2.3. Bunko

Bunko is a fast-paced and fun dice game that is purely based on luck.

* Key Benefit: It requires "absolutely no skill or previous experience" and is a fantastic way to socialize, as players rotate partners throughout the evening.
* Meeting Time: 2nd Monday of each month at 6:30 pm.
* Location: Retreat at Jordanelle Clubhouse.
* What to Bring: You bring a snack to share and your beverage of choice.

2.4. Canasta

The main object of this classic card game is to outscore the opposing team by forming melds (combinations of three or more cards of the same rank).

* Meeting Time: 1st and 3rd Mondays of the month, between 1:00 pm and 4:00 pm.
* Location: Park City Library.
* Membership: You must be a current Newcomers Member to play, but we offer a "one free trial" for potential members to check it out.

From the game table to the great outdoors, let's explore the groups that take advantage of our beautiful mountain setting.

3. Outdoor & Adventure Groups

Explore the stunning landscapes of the greater Park City area and enjoy some fresh air with these active and adventurous groups.

3.1. Bark Buddies

This is a fun and social weekly gathering for our members and their beloved "furry friends."

* Key Benefit: A wonderful opportunity for dogs to play and socialize while their owners connect and enjoy the outdoors.
* Meeting Time: Every Friday from 10:00 am to 11:00 am.
* Location: A local park or open space, with the specific location announced separately.

3.2. Downhill Skiing Groups

The club offers two distinct downhill skiing groups during the season, allowing members to hit the slopes at our world-class resorts.

* Deer Valley Group: Meets on Wednesdays at the Carpenter lift. The group includes several strong skiers who enjoy blue, double blue, and black runs. For those who prefer easier terrain, the leader will spend some time in the afternoon skiing greens and blues.
* PCMR Group: Meets weekly at Park City Mountain Resort. This group typically skis groomed blue and some black runs, and emphasizes that "everyone is welcome."

For both groups, schedules can be affected by snow conditions, so it is important to sign up for the weekly emails to get the latest updates.

3.3. Garden Group

This group is dedicated to promoting learning about the unique challenges and rewards of gardening in Park City's high desert environment.

* Vibe: Very informal, with a focus on members helping each other succeed.
* Meeting Time: Once a month during late spring and summer (dates vary).
* Activities: Meetings may include visiting a member's garden, hosting a speaker, going on a field trip, or participating in a hands-on activity.

3.4. Other Outdoor Groups

We have several other groups for those who love to get outside.

* Hiking
* Snowshoe Group

For the most up-to-date schedules and information on these activities, please check the monthly Newsletter.

After an adventure outside, you might be ready for a creative or social gathering indoors.

4. Creative & Social Pursuits

These groups are designed for members who enjoy reading, crafting, and unique social events that spark conversation and creativity.

4.1. Book Club

Our Book Club brings together a group of "bibliophiles who enjoy rousing discussions" on a wide variety of fiction and non-fiction books.

* Meeting Time: 10:00 am on the second Monday of each month.
* Location: Summit County Library in Kimball Junction or on Zoom.
* Special Traditions: The group enjoys a "Harvest Brunch" with a book exchange in November and holds an annual selection process to choose the books for the upcoming year.

4.2. CATS (Creating Art Together Socially)

CATS (Creating Art Together Socially) is a casual get-together for members to work on any art project or craft they choose.

* Key Benefit: It’s an opportunity to meet other creative people, share ideas, and be inspired. No sign-up is necessary; just come join the fun!
* Art Examples: The possibilities are endless, including jewelry, watercolor, fabric design, card creation, calligraphy, ceramics, drawing, and more.
* Meeting Time: 2nd and 4th Friday of each month.

4.3. JULIETS

JULIETS (Just Us Ladies Imbibing, Eating, Talking, Socializing!) is a casual monthly evening event for ladies to make friends and enjoy good conversation in the comfort of a member's home.

* What to Bring: Attendees bring a beverage and an appetizer to share.
* Good to Know: We ask that ladies attending consider hosting a future event.

4.4. Gone Wild

This group, open to both women and men, schedules a different and "unusual activity" about once a month.

* Vibe: The focus is on trying something new that you have probably never done before.
* Schedule: Events can be held any time—day, evening, or even overnight.
* Cost: Varies by activity.

4.5. Other Creative & Social Groups

5. Foodies & Friends: Dining Groups

Our dining groups are a fantastic way to meet other members in smaller, more intimate settings while enjoying delicious food and great conversation.

5.1. Cooking & Dining Adventures

This group is perfect for couples and singles who love to cook and share a meal in a more intimate setting.

* Format: Groups of 8 dine together once a month at a rotating member's home.
* How it Works: The host prepares the main course from a provided menu. Other members prepare and bring the appetizer, salad/soup, and dessert.
* What to Bring: In addition to their assigned dish, each member brings the wine or cocktail that has been suggested to accompany their course.
* Good to Know: Members who cannot host can sign up as an alternate to fill in when a regular member is unavailable.

5.2. Denim Dining

This is the club's "newest monthly activity," offering a themed, casual potluck in a member's home.

* Format: The event is limited to 8 participants. The host picks a theme (such as international cuisine, brunch, or a holiday) and coordinates the dishes.
* Key Difference: Unlike Cooking & Dining Adventures, participants get to choose which course they would like to prepare and bring within the host's chosen theme.
